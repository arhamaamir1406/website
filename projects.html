University of Toronto Robotics Association

Most of my Thursdays and Saturdays are spent doing work as the embedded engineer in the Autonomous Rover Team of the Robotics Association. Our team takes part in the annual Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition at Oakland where we race our rover against other competitors to pass through obstacles. I am currently working on Caffeine(the 2026 edition of our rover). My work encompasses both hardware and software components. The software side mainly requires me to work with ROS2, programming Arduinos, ESP32s and Raspberry PIs while the hardware side of things has me soldering, crimping, making sure of sustainable and feasible connections and the overall maintenance of electrical parts in the rover. Currently, I am working on CAN bus implentation and testing motor control by implementing PFM(Pulse Frequency Modulation) and PWM(Pulse Width Modulation).

GEN 1 Pokedex

This is a passion project I built over the weekend because I REALLY LOVE POKEMON. It is a recreation of the classic Generation 1 Pok√©dex using both a web interface and a physical Arduino controller. On the website end, it uses PokeAPI (evidentally, an online pokemon API) to get the data of all 151 generation 1 pokemon. Sources from github are used for the sprites and cries. On the hardware end, it uses an Arduino Uno with buttons, LEDs, a joystick and LCD to act as a physical controller to navigate the pokedex. More info on the github link below!!
